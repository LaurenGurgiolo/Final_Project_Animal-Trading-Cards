Turtle

Turtle shells are made mostly of bone; the upper part is the domed carapace, while the underside is the flatter plastron or belly-plate.

  • Naming and Etymology: The word turtle is borrowed from the French word tortue.
  • Size: The largest living species of turtle is the leatherback turtle, which can reach over 2.7 m.
  • Thermoregulation: Turtles, like other reptiles, have a limited ability to regulate their body temperature.
  • Diet and feeding: Most turtle species are opportunistic omnivores.

While popularly thought of as mute, turtles make various sounds to communicate. One study which recorded 53 species found that all of them vocalized. Tortoises may bellow when courting and mating.
